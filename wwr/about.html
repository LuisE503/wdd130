History


Rafting, as an adventure sport, has its origins in the 1950s in the United States and Europe. Although the practice of descending rivers in inflatable rafts dates back to ancient times, it was during this period that modern rafting began to take shape. Initially, explorers and fishermen used small rafts to navigate rivers with strong currents and aquatic slopes. Over time, this activity gained popularity and evolved into an extreme sport that attracts adventure enthusiasts from around the world.

Modern rafting is often done with high-tech inflatable rafts designed to withstand the powerful forces of fast-moving water. Clubs and tour operators began organizing trips for thrill-seekers, and the sport quickly grew in popularity. Today, rafting is enjoyed worldwide, from the Colorado River in the United States to the Zambezi River in Africa, offering both recreational fun and competitive challenges.
